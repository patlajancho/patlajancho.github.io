PHOTOS and FORMATS

+
We can paint a high-quality pastel portrait out of pretty much any photograph you send us as long as we can clearly see the details in the picture.

Still, from our experience, we recommend that you pay close attention to the following:

• Choose photos reflecting the photographed person's personality. Photos that say something to you and tell a story. Usually that story hides in the details. The expression, the pose, the eyes… The spark in the eyes... Everything counts to make a portrait truly special. If you have doubts about the way the person looks in the photo, if you think that the smile doesn't look natural, try again and pick another.

• A head-and-shoulders picture would work best. It’s strongly recommended the photo not to be full-length. It should instead be focusing on the face and showing just the upper part of people’s body. If you, however, still really want to order a portrait from a full-length photo, please use our Free Photo Consultation Service and we will give you our objective opinion.

• The lighting. Natural lighting and no sharp shadows on the face will make your photo look simply amazing.

+
Here are a few things to avoid when choosing your photographs:

• Low resolution. When painting a portrait we have to clearly see the details in the picture. Otherwise the person wouldn't look alike as much as if the picture was good-quality.

• Contrejour. This is a photographic technique in which the camera is pointing directly toward a source of light. It produces backlighting of the subject. This effects usually hides details.

• Bad angle. In most cases selfies don't work well on a portrait.

If there is a photo you really love, but you think it might not be suitable - it's alright, we are ready to assist you anytime! Just use our Free Photo Consultation Service and our professional artists will help you out. It might actually be good enough, so it's worth to try!

+
You can consult with our professional artists on any concerns you've got about your photo using our Free Photo Consultation Service.

Yup, you can! It is actually very common to do so. You simply submit separate reference photos and have them combined together into a single portrait. We could even paint you next to your favourite movie-character. Get creative!

+
The preferred file types for receiving your photos are .JPEG, .PNG, .BMP, or .TIFF. We suggest sending the highest resolution and quality photo available, no matter how big the file is. The better the quality of the original photo, the better the results will be.

Remember that we can only paint what we see on the photo provided by you. It is therefore important to provide a good quality photo with high resolution. Since Facebook for example reduces quality of pictures significantly, please don't download photos from social media if you have the original file.

+
Although we acknowledge that you could have some fairly distant memories you'd like to get painted or you might simply love the traditional film camera, we ourselves prefer to work with digital media. Therefore, we would kindly ask you to scan your printed photo so you could send it to us as a file.

The best medium to choose is, above all, a matter of personal taste. Our most popular medium is Black and White pastel. The unique thing about the Black & White technique is that it really well reflects the mood and person's true personality (or pet's cuteness!). After all, it is up to you to decide which one you like the most. Explore our galleries for inspiration and choose what fits you.
